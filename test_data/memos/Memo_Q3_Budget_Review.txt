I'm writing to share the Q3 2025 budget review results and outline our projections for Q4.

Q3 FINANCIAL HIGHLIGHTS:

REVENUE DRIVERS:
Our strongest growth came from three areas:
1. Azure NetApp Files deployments increased 22% QoQ as enterprise customers expanded storage
2. RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) solutions revenue grew 45% QoQ driven by the new Azure AI Foundry integration
3. Object Storage API adoption surged — 15 new customers adopted the S3-compatible endpoint for analytics workloads

EXPENSE HIGHLIGHTS:
- Cloud infrastructure (Azure): $87,500/month average — up 15% due to AI workload expansion
- Personnel: $142,000/month — added 2 ML engineers in August

- Software licenses: $8,200/month — Salesforce renewal at existing rate

AREAS OF CONCERN:
1. Azure AI costs are growing faster than revenue from AI services. We need to optimize our GPT-4o usage — consider implementing prompt caching and switching development workloads to GPT-4o-mini.
2. Staffing Agency costs for contract engineers ($14,700/month) should transition to full-time hires by Q1 2026.
3. Dell hardware purchases ($25,550 in Q3) need to be evaluated against Azure VM alternatives.
